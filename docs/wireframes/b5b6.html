49 · Photo etiquette training
← Photo guide
30 sec read

Photos that protect you and respect customers.

Good photos win disputes. Bad photos invade privacy. Here's the standard.

DO
Wide-angle, full room
Step back, capture the whole space. Shows your work in context.
Match before/after angles
Same spot, same framing. Comparison is the proof.
Good lighting
Turn on the lights. Open blinds. Don't backlight from windows.
Add extra rooms voluntarily
Document anything you're unsure about. More evidence = better protection.
DON'T
Photograph people
Never include the customer, kids, family, or anyone visible.
Close-ups of personal items
No mail, prescriptions, screens, paperwork, or photos on shelves.
Ignore skip-rooms
Customer-flagged rooms (offices, kids' rooms) are off-limits to your camera.
Edit or filter photos
Raw photos only. Edited images undermine the dispute system.
Why this matters
Photos are evidence. Customers who see disrespectful or careless photos lose trust in PureTask — and in you. Photos taken right protect both sides.
