## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you
visit. Similar technologies include **local storage**, **session
storage**, **pixels / web beacons**, **SDKs**, **device identifiers**,
and **session-recording / heatmap scripts**. We use these to make the
Platform work, to remember your preferences, to keep you signed in, to
measure performance, and (where you have consented) to support
marketing.

## 4. First-Party vs. Third-Party

- **First-party cookies** are set by puretask.co and our subdomains.
- **Third-party cookies** are set by service providers such as
  **Stripe** (payment fraud prevention), **Supabase** (auth/session),
  **Lovable Analytics** (usage analytics), and any advertising or
  session-recording vendors we engage. Their use is governed by their
  privacy policies.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Mobile SDKs and App Identifiers

Our mobile app uses SDKs that may store identifiers on your device:

- **OneSignal** --- push notifications (push token).
- **Stripe SDK** --- payment fraud prevention (device fingerprint).
- **Crash and performance** SDK (e.g., Sentry) --- error logs and stack
  traces.
- **OS-level advertising identifiers** --- **IDFA (iOS)** and **AAID
  (Android)**. We do **not** use these for advertising unless you opt
  in.

Manage notifications: **iOS** Settings → Notifications → PureTask;
**Android** Settings → Apps → PureTask → Notifications.

Manage advertising identifiers: **iOS** Settings → Privacy & Security →
Tracking → toggle off PureTask; **Android** Settings → Privacy → Ads →
Reset or Delete advertising ID.

## 6. Your Choices

### 6.1 Cookie Banner and Preferences Center

A cookie banner appears on first visit. A persistent **"Cookie
Preferences"** link is in the website footer. Update your choices any
time.

### 6.2 Browser Controls

Most browsers let you block or delete cookies. Disabling **strictly
necessary** cookies may break the Platform. - **Chrome**: Settings →
Privacy and security → Cookies. - **Safari**: Preferences → Privacy. -
**Firefox**: Settings → Privacy & Security. - **Edge**: Settings →
Cookies and site permissions.

### 6.3 Global Privacy Control (GPC)

We honor the **GPC** signal as an **opt-out of "sale" and "sharing"**
under CCPA/CPRA. When GPC is detected, we do not place advertising or
cross-context behavioral advertising cookies.

### 6.4 Do Not Track (DNT)

Because there is no industry consensus on DNT, we **do not currently
respond to DNT signals**. We do honor **GPC**. This statement is
provided under **California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA),
Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 22575**.

### 6.5 Mobile Device Settings

See Section 5 for iOS and Android instructions.

## 7. Withdrawing Consent

Withdraw consent for non-essential cookies at any time via: - The
**"Cookie Preferences"** link in the footer; - Browser settings (to
clear or block cookies); or - Enabling **GPC** in your browser.

Withdrawal does not affect lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
