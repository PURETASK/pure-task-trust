## (C) California Fair Chance Act (Gov. Code § 12952) and Cannabis Conviction Limits

PureTask is subject to the **California Fair Chance Act** and related employment-style fair-chance provisions, even though Pros are independent contractors, to the extent such provisions are extended to gig-economy relationships by regulation or case law. Under the Fair Chance Act and related California law:

- PureTask **will not** inquire about a conviction history before making an initial eligibility determination;
- PureTask's background-check vendor returns information only after the initial eligibility determination;
- **Marijuana-related convictions**: PureTask **will not** consider, ask about, or use against you any conviction, arrest, or detention for a non-felony marijuana-possession offense that occurred more than **two years ago**, consistent with **Cal. Lab. Code § 432.8** and **Cal. Gov. Code § 11362.45**. PureTask also **will not** consider, in connection with eligibility decisions, an applicant's **off-duty cannabis use** outside the workplace or any results of a non-psychoactive cannabis-metabolite test, consistent with **AB 2188** and **SB 700** (effective January 1, 2024);
- **Sealed, expunged, or dismissed convictions**: PureTask **will not** consider these against you (Cal. Lab. Code § 432.7);
- **Juvenile records**: not considered;
- **Arrests not leading to conviction**: not considered, with limited exceptions for currently pending matters.

If PureTask intends to take adverse action based on a conviction, you will receive a **Pre-Adverse Action Notice** with a copy of the report and a **Notice of Rights**, and a **5-business-day opportunity** to respond, dispute, or provide evidence of rehabilitation or mitigation, before any final adverse action is taken. After consideration of any response, PureTask will issue an **Adverse Action Notice** if it proceeds.

# PART 4 --- Summary of Your Rights

## (A) Summary of Your Rights Under the Federal Fair Credit Reporting Act

The federal **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)** promotes the accuracy, fairness, and privacy of information in the files of consumer reporting agencies. Your major rights under the FCRA include:

- **You must be told if information in your file has been used against you.** Anyone who uses a consumer report to take adverse action --- including denial of eligibility --- must tell you and give you the name, address, and phone number of the agency that provided the information.
- **You have the right to know what is in your file.** You may request and obtain all the information about you in the files of a consumer reporting agency (your "file disclosure"). In some cases, the disclosure is free.
- **You have the right to ask for a credit score.** (Not generally applicable to Pro screening.)
- **You have the right to dispute incomplete or inaccurate information.** The consumer reporting agency must investigate, usually within 30 days.
- **Consumer reporting agencies must correct or delete inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information.**
- **Consumer reporting agencies may not report outdated negative information.** Most negative information older than seven years may not be reported (ten years for bankruptcies).
- **Access to your file is limited** to people with a valid need.
- **You must give your consent** for reports to be provided for screening purposes.
- **You may limit "prescreened" offers** at 1-888-5-OPTOUT (1-888-567-8688).
- **You may seek damages from violators** in state or federal court.

For more information, contact the **Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)**: 1700 G Street NW, Washington, DC 20552; **consumerfinance.gov**.

## (B) Summary of California Rights Under Cal. Civ. Code § 1786.22

If an **investigative consumer report** is sought, you have the right to:

1.  **Visit the consumer reporting agency's offices** during normal business hours, with reasonable notice, to inspect the files maintained on you, accompanied by one other person of your choosing if desired.
2.  **Receive a copy of the report by certified mail** if you provide identification.
3.  **Receive a summary of the file by telephone**, upon request, if you provide proper identification.

The consumer reporting agency must provide trained personnel to explain to you any information in your file and must provide a written explanation of any coded information in your file. You have the right to dispute the completeness or accuracy of any item.

# Appendix A --- Pre-Adverse and Adverse Action Workflow (Operational Reference)

This appendix describes PureTask's pre-adverse / adverse action workflow. It is **not** part of the disclosure and authorization presented to applicants but is included here so that PureTask's operational practices are documented.

### Step 1 --- Receipt of Report

PureTask receives the consumer report from the background-check vendor. PureTask reviews any flagged items for accuracy, recency, and relevance.

### Step 2 --- Individualized Assessment (EEOC Guidance and Cal. Fair Chance Act)

For any conviction or other flagged item, PureTask conducts an individualized assessment considering:

- Nature and gravity of the offense;
- Time elapsed since offense, sentence, or release;
- Nature of the Platform role (cleaning in private homes);
- Evidence of rehabilitation or mitigation;
- Whether the conviction is excluded under California Fair Chance Act / SB 1480 / AB 2188 / SB 700;
- Any evidence the applicant provides.

### Step 3 --- Pre-Adverse Action Notice (FCRA § 1681b(b)(3) and Cal. Civ. Code § 1786.40)

If PureTask is considering adverse action, PureTask provides the applicant with:

- A **Pre-Adverse Action Notice** (separate template, "07A");
- A **copy of the consumer report**;
- The **Summary of Rights Under the FCRA**;
- For California applicants, the **California Fair Chance Act Notice**;
- A **5-business-day opportunity** (longer for California) to respond, dispute, or provide evidence.

### Step 4 --- Wait Period

PureTask waits at least **5 business days** (and longer if the applicant requests an extension) before taking adverse action, to allow the applicant to:

- Dispute inaccuracies with the consumer reporting agency directly;
- Provide context, mitigation, or rehabilitation evidence;
- Respond to the substance of the flagged information.

### Step 5 --- Adverse Action Notice (if proceeding)

If, after considering the applicant's response, PureTask proceeds with adverse action, PureTask provides the applicant with:

- An **Adverse Action Notice** (separate template, "07B");
- A **copy of the consumer report** (if not previously provided or if updated);
- The **Summary of Rights Under the FCRA**;
- The name, address, and phone number of the consumer reporting agency;
- A statement that the consumer reporting agency did not make the decision;
- A notice that the applicant has the right to a free copy of the report from the CRA within 60 days, and the right to dispute its accuracy.

### Step 6 --- Recordkeeping

PureTask retains:

- The signed Disclosure and Authorization;
- The consumer report received;
- Any internal assessment notes;
- Any applicant response;
- Any pre-adverse and adverse action notices sent;

For **at least 5 years** after the date of the action (or longer if required by applicable law).
