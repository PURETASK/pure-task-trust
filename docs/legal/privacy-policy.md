## 4. Sensitive Personal Information

The CCPA/CPRA defines certain data as "**Sensitive Personal Information
(SPI)**." For Pros, this includes the last 4 of SSN or Tax ID (for IRS
reporting), precise geolocation (during active check-ins), and
government-issued ID.

PureTask uses SPI **only for purposes that are reasonably necessary** to
provide the Platform: identity verification, tax reporting, fraud
prevention, and trust and safety. We do not use SPI to infer
characteristics about Users, for advertising, or for any purpose
unrelated to those listed. Accordingly, under Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.121,
the right to limit use of SPI **does not apply** to our current
processing. You may still submit a request to limit; we will respond as
required by law. We will reassess this position annually.

## 5. How We Use Personal Information

We use personal information to:

1.  **Provide the Platform** --- create accounts, match Clients with
    Pros, process bookings and payments, deliver notifications.
2.  **Verify identity, trust, and safety** --- confirm Pro eligibility,
    perform background and watchlist screening, detect fraud, prevent
    abuse.
3.  **Process payments and payouts** --- facilitate Stripe Connect
    charges, application fees, and tax forms.
4.  **Communicate** --- send transactional messages, service
    announcements, and (with consent where required) marketing.
5.  **Personalize and improve** --- tailor matching, recommendations,
    and content.
6.  **Analytics and research** --- measure usage, debug, develop new
    features.
7.  **Legal and compliance** --- comply with laws, respond to legal
    process, enforce Terms, protect rights, property, and safety.

### 5.1 Automated Decision-Making and Profiling

The Platform uses automated processes to: - **Match Clients with Pros**
based on availability, service area, ratings, and Client-stated
preferences; - **Detect fraud** (unusual login patterns, payment
anomalies); - **Compute Reliability Indicators** from neutral signals
(on-time arrival, completion rate, dispute history).

These automated processes are **not** used for decisions that produce
legal or similarly significant effects (e.g., we do not auto-deny Pro
applications without human review of background-check results). You may
request human review of any matching or fraud-detection outcome by
emailing **otherpuretask@gmail.com**.

### 5.2 Legal Bases (where applicable)

Where the **GDPR or UK GDPR** applies (we do not currently market in
EEA/UK and geo-block where feasible), legal bases include contract
performance, legitimate interests (fraud prevention, product
improvement), consent (marketing emails, precise location), and legal
obligation.

To exercise, email **otherpuretask@gmail.com** with subject "**CCPA
Request**." We verify identity (typically by confirming account details)
and respond within **45 days** (extendable by 45 with notice).

### 10.3 California "Shine the Light" (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.83)

California residents who provided personal information to PureTask in
connection with the Platform may request --- once per year, at no charge

parties' direct marketing purposes** during the prior calendar year.
**PureTask does not currently disclose personal information to third

Shine the Light request, email **otherpuretask@gmail.com**, subject
"**Shine the Light Request**."

### 10.4 Texas Residents (TDPSA)

You have the right to: - **Confirm** processing; - **Access** your
data; - **Correct** inaccuracies; - **Delete** personal data; -
**Portable copy** in machine-readable format; - **Opt out** of (a)
targeted advertising, (b) sale, and (c) profiling with legal or
similarly significant effects; - **Appeal** a denial.

Email **otherpuretask@gmail.com**, subject "**TDPSA Request**." We

## 11. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed
to protect personal information, including encryption in transit (TLS
1.2+), encryption at rest for sensitive fields (including last-4 of
SSN/Tax ID and government-ID images), role-based access control,
row-level security, secret rotation, audit logging, and least-privilege
access. We require service providers to sign **Data Processing Addendums
(DPAs)** with appropriate confidentiality and security obligations.

No system is 100% secure. You are responsible for safeguarding your
credentials.

**Breach notification.** If your personal information is compromised, we
will notify you and applicable regulators within timeframes required by
**Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.82** (without unreasonable delay), **Tex. Bus. &
Com. Code § 521.053** (no later than 60 days after determination),
**Fla. Stat. § 501.171** (within 30 days), and other applicable laws.
