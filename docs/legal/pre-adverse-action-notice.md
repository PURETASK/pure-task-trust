# PureTask --- Pre-Adverse Action Notice (Template)

**Document 07A --- operational template, not part of applicant signup flow**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

> **HOW TO USE THIS TEMPLATE**
>
> This is a **Pre-Adverse Action Notice** required by the federal **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), 15 U.S.C. § 1681b(b)(3)**, the **California Investigative Consumer Reporting Agencies Act, Cal. Civ. Code § 1786.40**, and analogous Texas and Florida law.
>
> Send this notice to a Pro applicant **before** taking adverse action (denial, suspension, deactivation) based wholly or in part on a consumer report or investigative consumer report.
>
> **Required attachments** with this notice: 1. A complete copy of the consumer report obtained; 2. The **CFPB Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act** (the official short-form document); 3. For California applicants, a copy of the **California ICRAA Summary of Rights** under Cal. Civ. Code § 1786.22.
>
> **Wait at least 5 business days** between sending this notice and taking final adverse action. Longer waits are advisable in California and for complex disputes. The applicant must have a meaningful opportunity to respond, dispute, or provide context.
>
> **Delivery**: Send by email to the address on file, with a delivery / read receipt enabled where possible. Retain proof of sending in the applicant's file for at least 5 years. If applicant requests paper, mail by first-class with proof of mailing.
>
> **California applicants**: Be especially mindful of the California Fair Chance Act (Gov. Code § 12952), AB 2188 / SB 700 (cannabis-related), and Cal. Lab. Code § 432.7 (sealed/expunged/dismissed convictions). If the basis for adverse action is a conviction, complete an **Individualized Assessment** before sending this notice.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Your Rights and Next Steps

You have at least **five (5) business days** from the date of this notice --- until **\[DEADLINE DATE\]** --- to:

- **Review** the attached consumer report carefully;
- **Dispute** the accuracy or completeness of any information in the report directly with the consumer reporting agency listed above (you may also notify us);
- **Respond** to us with any context, mitigation, rehabilitation evidence, or explanation you wish us to consider;
- **Request additional time** if the response window is insufficient for you to obtain documentation or counsel --- we will consider reasonable extension requests in good faith;
- **Request a free copy** of your file directly from the consumer reporting agency, which you are entitled to receive (in most cases) within a few business days.

To respond to PureTask, please reply to this email at **otherpuretask@gmail.com** with the subject line "**Pre-Adverse Action Response --- \[Your Name\]**."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### What Happens Next

After the response period:

- **If you respond**, we will review your response and supporting documentation before making any final decision.
- **If you do not respond**, or if after reviewing your response we still intend to take adverse action, you will receive a separate **Adverse Action Notice** explaining the final decision and your continuing rights.
- **If we decide not to take adverse action**, we will let you know and complete the Pro signup process.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### California Applicants --- Additional Information

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under California law:

- We will conduct (or have conducted) an **Individualized Assessment** of any conviction-related information, considering the nature and gravity of the offense, the time elapsed, the nature of the role, and any evidence of rehabilitation you provide;
- We will **not consider**: (a) any non-felony marijuana-possession conviction more than two years old; (b) your off-duty cannabis use; (c) results of any non-psychoactive cannabis-metabolite test; (d) any conviction that has been sealed, expunged, dismissed, or set aside; (e) any juvenile record; (f) most arrests not leading to conviction;
- These protections come from the **California Fair Chance Act (Gov. Code § 12952)**, **Cal. Lab. Code § 432.7**, **Cal. Lab. Code § 432.8**, **AB 2188**, and **SB 700**.

If you believe we are considering information that we are prohibited from considering, please tell us in your response.
