## 1. Two Types of Messages

PureTask uses text messaging for two purposes. You can consent to each independently.

### 1.1 Transactional / Service Messages (Required for Bookings)

These are messages **necessary to operate your account and bookings**. Examples:

- Booking confirmations;
- Pro assignment notifications (Clients) or new booking offers (Pros);
- Reminders before scheduled service;
- Arrival, completion, and review-window notifications;
- Payment receipts and payout notifications;
- Dispute updates;
- Security alerts (e.g., new device login);
- Two-factor authentication codes.

By creating an account and providing your mobile number, you consent to receive transactional messages from PureTask. **This consent is necessary to use the Platform.**

### 1.2 Marketing / Promotional Messages (Optional, Express Written Consent Required)

These are messages **promoting features, offers, referrals, or other marketing content**. Examples:

- New feature announcements;
- Promotional offers and discounts;
- Referral program invitations;
- Newsletter content;
- Re-engagement messages.

Under the TCPA, marketing text messages sent using automated technology require **prior express written consent**. This consent is **not** a condition of using the Platform.

## 3. Quiet Hours and Banned Days

### 3.1 Federal TCPA Quiet Hours
Consistent with **47 C.F.R. § 64.1200(c)(1)**, PureTask will not send **marketing text messages** before **8:00 a.m.** or after **9:00 p.m.** in your **local time zone** (determined by your area code or last known location).

### 3.2 Transactional Messages
Transactional messages (booking confirmations, arrival alerts, two-factor codes, security alerts) are **not** subject to quiet-hour restrictions, as they are not solicitations under the TCPA and are tied to your active use of the Platform.

### 3.3 Florida Restrictions (FTSA)
Under the **Florida Telephone Solicitation Act, Fla. Stat. § 501.059(1)(c)**, marketing telephonic sales calls and text messages may not be made:

- **Before 8:00 a.m. or after 8:00 p.m. local time** (Florida quiet hours are stricter than federal);
- On **any Sunday**, unless the recipient has expressly agreed to Sunday messaging;
- More than **three (3) times per 24-hour period** for the same subject matter;
- **Without disclosing the sender’s identity** at the start of the message.

PureTask’s marketing-SMS system is configured to comply with FTSA for Florida-based numbers.

### 3.4 California
California does not impose additional quiet-hour rules beyond TCPA, but **California Public Utilities Code § 2871** requires accurate caller-identification information. See Section 5.

## 6. Opting Out

### 6.1 Opt Out of Marketing Messages

Opt out at any time:

- **Reply STOP** to any marketing text message;
- **Adjust preferences** in the PureTask app: Settings → Notifications → Marketing SMS → toggle off;
- **Email otherpuretask@gmail.com** with the subject “**SMS Opt Out**.”

A confirmation message will be sent. No further marketing messages will be sent after opt-out.

### 6.2 Opt Out of Transactional Messages
To stop transactional messages, close your PureTask account, as transactional messages are necessary to operate Bookings safely. Close your account in settings or by emailing **otherpuretask@gmail.com**.

### 6.3 Re-Subscribing
Re-subscribe in account settings or by texting **START** to PureTask’s shortcode.

### 6.4 Time to Honor Opt-Out
PureTask honors opt-out requests within **ten (10) business days** as required by federal and state law. In most cases, opt-out is processed immediately upon receipt of STOP.

## 13. California, Texas, and Florida Specific Notices

### California
California Public Utilities Code and the **California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act** apply to text messaging. You have the right to opt out of any “sale” or “sharing” of your phone number for cross-context behavioral advertising, as described in the Privacy Policy. PureTask honors the **Global Privacy Control (GPC)** signal as an opt-out.

### Texas
Under the **Texas Business and Commerce Code Ch. 305** and TCPA, you have the right to revoke consent to marketing messages at any time. PureTask will honor revocation requests within ten (10) business days.

### Florida
Under the **Florida Telephone Solicitation Act (FTSA), Fla. Stat. § 501.059**, marketing telephonic sales calls and text messages require **prior express written consent**, and are subject to the quiet-hour and frequency restrictions in Section 3.3. PureTask’s marketing-SMS consent flow is designed to comply with FTSA. You may revoke consent at any time.
