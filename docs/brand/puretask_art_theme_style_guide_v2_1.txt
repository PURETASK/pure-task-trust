1. Scope and Intended Use
This guide defines the official visual system for PureTask across product, onboarding, booking flows, marketing, motion,
social media, print, and implementation handoff. It is designed to keep the PureTask brand consistent as the company
grows from concept-stage identity into a trustworthy service platform.
Use this document as an operating standard, not as a loose mood board. When a team member must choose between
a visually interesting option and a clearer, more trustworthy option, PureTask should always choose the clearer, more
trustworthy option.
Primary users
Product designers, brand designers, marketers, illustrators,
motion designers, developers, agencies, and future
collaborators.
Applies to
App UI, web UI, onboarding flows, booking flow, ads, social
content, decks, print collateral, uniforms, and support
materials.
Decision rule Every visual choice should increase trust, reduce friction, and
make the service feel easier to understand.
2. Brand Essence and Positioning
PureTask is a premium home-services platform that should feel like clean service + trusted technology + effortless
execution. The brand should communicate that life gets easier, details get organized, and the user can move forward
with confidence.
PureTask is not a discount maid-service brand, not a cluttered marketplace, and not a cold operations dashboard. It
should feel premium, human, calm, practical, and operationally strong.
PureTask is Premium but practical; modern but human; efficient but calm;
polished without feeling fake.
PureTask is not Cheap, chaotic, goofy, over-corporate, generic, or mascot-first.
Emotional outcomes Relief, trust, clarity, confidence, calm momentum.
Brand shorthand A polished promise: fast, clear, premium, and under control.

4. Brand Architecture and Asset Hierarchy
PureTask works best as a system with shared DNA, not as one overloaded logo. The brand should separate the
company mark, the companion asset, and the shared motifs so each can play a clear role.
Primary mark
The glossy P-house mark is the company anchor. Use it for app
icon, navigation, billing, legal, invoices, trust badges, print
identifiers, and small-format brand recognition.
Companion asset
Dash the hummingbird adds motion, care, precision, and
delight. Use Dash in onboarding, loading, success states,
selected campaign art, and premium storytelling moments.
Shared motifs Sparkle, motion trail, white highlights, blue-cyan gradients,
rounded depth, and soft glow connect the system.
Hierarchy rule The logo leads official identity. Dash supports emotional
richness. Shared motifs tie everything together.
 Logo only: navigation, app icon, invoices, legal, trust-focused surfaces, uniforms.
 Dash only: onboarding delight, social storytelling, motion moments, hero illustrations.
 Logo + Dash: homepage hero, premium campaigns, splash screens, keynote visuals.
 Do not force Dash into every logo instance, and do not let the mascot outrank the company mark.
5. Typography System
PureTask Art Theme & Art Style Guide V2.1
Typography controls tone, structure, and trust. PureTask typography should feel modern, clear, premium, and highly
readable. It should never feel gimmicky, flimsy, or overly futuristic.
Recommendation: use one primary sans-serif family across product and most brand use. Inter is the strongest default
recommendation for product and digital surfaces because it is readable at UI sizes and scales cleanly. Use a system
fallback stack when needed. If a display accent is desired in campaigns, use it sparingly and keep it in the same modern
sans family lane.

7. Shape Language, Spacing, and Elevation
PureTask Art Theme & Art Style Guide V2.1
PureTask forms should feel polished, comfortable, and easy to read at a glance. Rounded confidence is central to the
system. Shapes should feel safe to engage with, slightly buoyant, and intentionally simplified.
Corner radius family 8, 12, 16, 24, 32. Use smaller radii for compact controls and
larger radii for cards, hero surfaces, and onboarding shells.
Spacing scale
4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64. Product spacing should feel
generous enough to look premium but tight enough to stay
operational.
Silhouette rule Prefer broad readable shapes, soft tapering, and clean
negative space. Avoid jagged or hostile geometry.
Elevation tiers Tier 1 subtle: 0 2 8 rgba(7,42,85,.08); Tier 2 medium: 0 8 24
rgba(7,42,85,.10); Tier 3 hero: 0 14 36 rgba(7,42,85,.16).
 Do not let text sit too close to control edges.
 Use consistent internal padding in cards and tables.
 Premium feel comes from clean spacing and hierarchy, not from cramming more detail into a smaller space.
8. Material and Lighting System
PureTask blends soft 3D polish with app-native refinement. The material language should support trust and finish, not
novelty. Use depth intentionally so the brand feels elevated but still usable.
Material tier 1: Hero gloss
Use for homepage hero art, campaigns, concept sheets, and
premium storytelling visuals. Stronger gradients and richer
highlights are acceptable here.
Material tier 2: Product gloss
Use for icon treatments, onboarding art, confirmation states,
and selected branded UI moments. Keep depth lighter than
hero art.
Material tier 3: Flat fallback Use for print, embroidery, small icons, monochrome surfaces,
and reproduction-constrained environments.
Lighting behavior
Favor bright upper surfaces, soft rim light, controlled
reflections, and clean optimistic contrast. Never let the light
feel greasy, dirty, or dramatic by default.

9. Dash the Hummingbird: Model and Usage Rules
Dash is PureTask's companion character and motion symbol. Dash should signal precision, speed, attentiveness, and
lightly optimistic service energy without turning the brand into a cartoon.
Dash should always remain recognizable by a sleek hummingbird silhouette, rounded aerodynamic body, elegant
pointed beak, intelligent eye treatment, and wing geometry that feels directional and refined.
Approved emotional range Focused, attentive, confident, graceful, calm, lightly uplifting.
Disallowed emotional range Goofy, meme-like, slapstick, angry, sleepy, chaotic, juvenile.
Core variants Hero Dash, onboarding Dash, micro-icon Dash, silhouette
Dash, motion-trail Dash, print-safe Dash.
Proportion guidance Keep the head compact, body rounded but not puffy, beak
elegant not aggressive, and wings readable at small sizes.
Animation logic
Dash may glide, sweep, lead the eye, resolve into sparkle, or
support a success-state transition. Dash should not bounce
wildly or dominate every interface.

Dash Usage Matrix
 Use Dash in onboarding, loading, confirmation, social storytelling, campaign hero art, and selected success moments.
 Reduce Dash detail for small contexts. Preserve silhouette first.
 Do not distort Dash proportions, add comic facial expressions, or cover core product screens with mascot clutter.
10. Iconography System
PureTask icons should look like small members of the same family as the logo: rounded, readable, stable, and slightly
polished. Icons must prioritize silhouette first and detail second.
Grid logic
Use a consistent icon grid and consistent corner radius
behavior. Product icons should feel cohesive before they feel
expressive.
Construction Favor filled or hybrid-filled icons for core navigation and
service concepts. Use outlines sparingly and consistently.
Line weight Avoid ultra-thin strokes. Icons should remain readable at small
size and high-density UI conditions.
Core families Home, booking, calendar, time, location, trust, payment,
support, extras, cleaning focus areas, pets, reliability.
 Do not mix multiple unrelated icon styles in product.
 Do not rely on tiny interior detail that disappears at small sizes.
 Keep icon metaphors simple and consistent.
11. Product Expression System
PureTask product design should feel like a guided concierge flow rather than a generic form-builder interface. The
product must reduce uncertainty, communicate progress, and make the user feel that everything is under control.
Page Shell and Layout
 Use a soft blue-gray or near-white page canvas with subtle ambient glow.
 Favor centered step-based layouts for onboarding and booking.
 Use one primary card per step whenever possible.
 Reserve right-side summary panels for review, price, and trust-critical contexts.
 Keep top chrome minimal during focused flows.
Onboarding and Booking Flow Rules

Progress behavior
Every major step should show visible progress with step count
and percent or equivalent. The user should always know
where they are.
Input grouping Only ask for one category of information per step. Do not
overload a single screen with unrelated questions.
Trust moments
Price summaries, holds, approvals, actual hours, and next
steps must be visually clear and intentionally framed.
Repeat use
Store home profile data and preferences so future bookings
feel faster and smarter.
Reference Product Direction
The current PureTask reference screens already point in the right direction: centered onboarding cards, progress bars,
strong titles, helper text, structured address capture, access instructions, cleaning preferences, and a review surface

Reference layout patterns: home-details capture, access notes, preferences, and review/price-summary behavior.

13. Motion and Interaction System
Motion in PureTask should reinforce clarity, confidence, and premium flow. Motion is not decorative filler. It should
guide the eye, show change, and reward completion without becoming noisy.
Core motion verbs Glide, sweep, trail, resolve, settle.
Timing guidance
Micro hover 120-180 ms; control transitions 180-260 ms;
card/page reveals 280-420 ms; confirmation moments 400-
700 ms.
Easing Prefer soft ease-out and smooth deceleration. Avoid spring
chaos unless carefully tuned.

Dash motion Use Dash for premium moments such as onboarding, loading,
or confirmation. Keep Dash brief and elegant.
State feedback
Buttons, chips, and summaries should show clean
hover/focus/active feedback so the interface feels alive and
legible.
14. Accessibility and Responsive Behavior
Accessibility is part of the PureTask trust promise. Premium design is incomplete if users cannot read, navigate, or
understand it easily.
Contrast
Critical content such as prices, totals, dates, CTA labels, and
warnings must meet readable contrast standards.
Focus states
Every interactive control needs a visible keyboard focus
treatment. Hover cannot be the only signal.
Touch targets Buttons, chips, toggles, and selectors must remain comfortable
on mobile and tablet.
Reduced motion Use simplified motion where users prefer less animation.
Avoid making essential feedback animation-dependent.
Responsive rules
Desktop may use centered cards + sticky summary. Tablet
reduces columns. Mobile stacks into a single flow with large
tap targets and collapsed summaries.
 Do not use color alone to indicate error, success, or state.
 Do not shrink important controls below practical touch size.
 Keep helper text readable and close to the control it explains.
15. Print, Physical Reproduction, and Simplified Marks
PureTask will need to work beyond screens. Reproduction rules should preserve recognizability even when gloss, glow,
and gradients are removed.
Required variants Full-color premium mark, flat-color mark, monochrome mark,
dark-background mark, embroidery-safe mark.
Simplification rule Preserve silhouette and structure first. Remove glow and
complex highlight behavior before sacrificing recognizability.

Physical use cases Uniforms, leave-behinds, cards, stickers, signage, printed

Minimum standard
Small-format use should remain crisp and readable. If the
premium treatment muddies the mark, switch to simplified
output.
16. Implementation, Governance, and Handoff
A strong visual system only scales if the organization knows how to maintain it. Implementation quality is part of brand
quality.
Source of truth
Maintain one current design-system source for marks, Dash
variants, gradients, tokens, components, and approved
exports.
Asset packaging Provide SVG, PNG, PDF, transparent versions, dark-background
variants, and simplified print variants.
Naming conventions File names should identify asset, variant, color mode, and
version clearly.
Approval path Major brand changes should be reviewed against this guide
before adoption across product or marketing.
AI-generated assets All AI-assisted outputs should be reviewed for geometry drift,
color drift, mascot drift, and trust-tone consistency before use.
Recommended Handoff Package
 Logo pack: primary, simplified, monochrome, dark-background, print-safe.
 Dash pack: hero, onboarding, small icon, silhouette, motion key art.
 Token pack: colors, spacing, radii, shadows, motion timings, typography roles.
 Component pack: buttons, inputs, chips, summaries, trust banners, service cards, progress bars.
 Reference pack: approved screenshots and hero examples.
17. Expanded Do / Don't Guidance
Do Don't
Keep the palette tight and the gradients smooth. Do not introduce random colors that weaken blue-cyan
ownership.
PureTask Art Theme & Art Style Guide V2.1
Use sparkle as a sign of completion or refined delight. Do not use sparkle as visual confetti.
Let the logo stay simple and recognizable. Do not force Dash into every mark.
Use Dash to add motion and warmth in selected moments. Do not make Dash goofy, juvenile, or louder than the company
mark.
Design every screen so the user feels under control. Do not crowd forms or hide trust-critical information.
Use glow to support emphasis and premium finish. Do not flood the interface with glow until readability suffers.
Build environments that feel bright, believable, and calm. Do not drift into generic maid-service clichés or cold B2B sterility.
18. Final Positioning Statement
PureTask should always look like a polished promise: fast, clear, premium, reassuring, and fully in control. The brand
should make people feel that help is already organized, details are already handled, and service will be delivered with
confidence and care.
Every visual choice in PureTask should strengthen trust, reduce friction, and make booking and managing home service
feel easier. If a design choice weakens that outcome, it is off-brand.
